Product Design Approach

To break into a saturated SaaS market, I leaned on Lean UX. Why? Because it let me prototype fast, validate with data, and adapt quickly—critical when the product had to be launched, tested, and deliver measurable outcomes within just four months.

Analyzing competitor gaps

  • Competitive analysis for differentiation: Scanned a saturated boilerplate SaaS market to extract user pain points and gaps—mining feedback on X, Product Hunt, Dev Tools, Uneed, and Gumroad to identify what would make us stand out.
  • Constraint → opportunity:The only stakeholder-backed differentiator was AI integration (concept-stage). I defined the AI value proposition and its UX from scratch so it could credibly anchor positioning.
  • Synthesis with AI assistance: Used AI tools to cluster comments/data and prioritize jobs-to-be-done—turning noisy market chatter into clear themes (trust, time-to-setup, billing clarity, docs).
  • Two design strategies: AI-first, USP-led route with a distinctive visual signature to pop in listings; (B) balanced, familiar route highlighting all core features to lower friction and boost conversion.

User Feedbacks

The first design approach was not taken well by users globally. useSAASkit was failing to stand out from the competitors.

Conducted surveys and tracked user behavior to uncover friction points and identify features failing to support user success.

Platform Audit

  • What we found: The first version felt unfamiliar. New users didn’t trust new brands, and our AI value wasn’t clear.
  • Make it familiar: I rebuilt the flow to match patterns users already know from top competitors—so it felt easy and safe.
  • Keep our edge: I placed the AI help inside those familiar steps (setup, docs, pricing), so it’s useful and clearly different.

Engineering Handoff & Compliance

  • Design handoff: Delivered a full design system (tokens + components) and a clean Figma file—each flow sectioned, variants/states documented, specs/redlines included.
  • Build partnership: Worked with Engineering + QA on acceptance criteria and testing; shipped lightweight micro-interactions (skeleton loaders, smooth search results) without hurting performance.
  • Quality gate: Dogfooded on staging across devices/browsers; fixed issues fast; signed off only after reliable end-to-end journeys.
  • Compliance: GDPR data-minimization + RBAC; localization for DE/NL/FR/EN (expansion-tested); WCAG 2.1 AA—focus order, 44×44 targets, ≥4.5:1 contrast, ARIA landmarks.
